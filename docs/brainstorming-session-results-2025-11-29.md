## Technique Sessions

### Technique 1: Alien Anthropologist (Theatrical)

**Goal:** Look at video collaboration tools with fresh, outsider eyes

**Key Observations:**

1. **The Pointing Paradox**
   - Humans have evolved a precise "finger" appendage for indicating objects
   - In physical space, pointing is instant and universal
   - Screen-sharing tools force humans to abandon this natural gesture
   - Result: Verbose verbal navigation ("left... no YOUR left... up a bit...")

2. **Verbal Coordinate Systems**
   - Enormous cognitive load translating visual positions into words
   - "The Same Page Tax" - massive overhead to establish shared visual focus
   - Inefficient: describing positions that could be shown in milliseconds

3. **Tool vs. Natural Behavior Mismatch**
   - Zoom/Teams: Show faces prominently, make pointing nearly impossible
   - Miro: Cursors exist but on separate whiteboard, not actual work artifacts
   - Workarounds exist but are paid/closed-source

**Core Insight: "Writing on Glass"**
- The ideal annotation feels like writing on glass over the content
- Clear, fast, ephemeral, non-destructive
- Not a separate whiteboard (Miro) or clunky markup (Zoom)
- Transparent overlay that's immediate, like breath on glass

**Differentiation Opportunity:**
- Open-source + self-hosted + "finger pointing" = unique position
- No existing tool combines: natural pointing + open + self-hosted

### Technique 3: First Principles Thinking (Creative)

**Goal:** Strip away all learned assumptions and rebuild from fundamental truths

**Irreducible Core Needs:**
1. Two or more people need to look at the same visual thing
2. They need to communicate about specific parts of it
3. They need to know what the other person is focused on
4. It needs to happen in real-time (synchronous)

**First Principles Questions & Answers:**

| Question | First Principles Answer |
|----------|------------------------|
| Simplest way to show "where I'm looking"? | Share x,y coordinates |
| Minimum viable "draw attention"? | Point at it - the finger |
| What to leave OUT? | Shapes - circles, rectangles, arrows |

**The Two Primitives:**

| Primitive | Behavior | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Laser pointer** | Glowing dot with trail | Ephemeral attention - "look HERE right now" |
| **Finger drawing** | Freehand strokes | Persistent annotation - natural, like tracing on glass |

**Multi-User First Principles:**

| Aspect | Decision | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Colors | Auto-assigned unique color per person | No setup, instant identity |
| Concurrency | Everyone draws simultaneously | No turn-taking, no "who has the pen?" |
| Visibility | All pointers visible to all | Natural, like physical collaboration |

## Action Planning

### Top 3 Priority Ideas (The MVP)

#### #1 Priority: Laser Pointer with Glow + Trail

- **Rationale:** The most fundamental "pointing finger" experience - instant, ephemeral attention
- **What it does:** Glowing dot that follows cursor, with fading trail showing movement
- **Design north star:** Should feel like pointing with a laser pointer in a presentation

#### #2 Priority: Finger Drawing (Freehand Strokes)

- **Rationale:** Persistent annotation for circling, underlining, connecting - "writing on glass" realized
- **What it does:** Freehand drawing that persists during the session
- **Design north star:** Should feel like drawing on glass with your finger - smooth, immediate, natural

#### #3 Priority: Multi-User Simultaneous Drawing

- **Rationale:** True collaboration - everyone draws at once, no friction
- **What it does:** Auto-assigned unique colors, all participants see all pointers/drawings, no permission modes
- **Design north star:** Should feel like multiple people standing at the same whiteboard
